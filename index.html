About the program

Training laboratory professionals
for Northern Luzon

The FNLGHTC Pathology Internship Program is a Commission on Higher Education (CHED)-recognized internship designed for Medical Technology students completing their clinical training hours under Republic Act 5527.

Interns rotate through all major sections of the clinical laboratory under the direct supervision of licensed Medical Technologists and Pathologists.

The program runs for six months, structured to fulfill 832 of the 1664 clinical training hours required for board examination eligibility, with per-section evaluation and a final competency assessment.

CHED-accredited training site

Recognized clinical training affiliate for Medical Technology programs across the Ilocos and Cagayan Valley regions.

Supervised by seasoned medical professionals

All rotations are directly supervised by licensed medical technologists and pathologists.

Case-based learning

Interns encounter real clinical specimens and participate in case discussions, preparing them for actual hospital laboratory environments.

Complete documentation support

Training forms, competency checklists, and evaluation records provided in coordination with partner universities.

Training areas

Eight core laboratory
rotations

Each section is structured to build specific competencies. Interns complete all eight rotations before final evaluation, ensuring comprehensive exposure across the clinical laboratory.

01
Hematology
150 hours

Complete blood counts, peripheral blood smear interpretation, coagulation studies, and blood banking procedures.

02
Clinical Chemistry
150 hours

Enzymatic assays, metabolic panels, electrolyte analysis, liver and kidney function tests using automated analyzers.

03
Microbiology & Parasitology
125 hours

Culture and sensitivity testing, gram staining, parasitological identification, and infection control protocols.

04
Clinical Microscopy & Parasitology
100 hours

Routine and special urinalysis, CSF and pleural fluid analysis, microscopic examination of urine sediment.

05
Serology & Immunology
110 hours

Rapid diagnostic tests, ELISA methods.

06
Blood Bank
100 hours

ABO/Rh blood typing and cross-matching.

07
Histopathology Technique & Cytology
50 hours

Histopathologic slide preparation, gross specimen examination, cytology smear preparation under direct pathologist supervision.

08
Laboratory Management
47 hours

Phlebotomy procedures, specimen collection, and handling.

How to apply

Your path to the program

1

Confirm eligibility

Check with your university dean or clinical instructor that FNLGHTC is an accredited affiliate site for your school's MedTech program.

2

Submit a letter of intent

Send a formal letter of intent from your school addressed to the Chief Medical Technologist, Department of Pathology, FNLGHTC.

3

Complete pre-placement requirements

Submit medical clearance, hepatitis B vaccination record, NBI clearance, and government-issued ID to the Pathology Department.

4

Attend orientation

Scheduled prior to your first rotation date. Orientation covers hospital policies, laboratory safety, and your rotation schedule.

5

Begin your rotations

Report to the Pathology Department on your designated start date. Your supervisor for each rotation will guide you from day one.

Pre-placement requirements

  • Original letter of endorsement from your university signed by the Dean
  • Completed Personal Data Sheet
  • Photocopy of Transcript of Records
  • Medical certificate with accompanying CBC, urinalysis, fecalysis, chest x-ray, anti-HIV screening, HbsAg, quantitative anti-HBs, and drug test results
  • Certificate of Good Moral Character
  • Proof of vaccination against hepatitis B and Flu
  • Latest 2×2 ID photos (2 copies, white background)
